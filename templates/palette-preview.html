Full 7-category swell palette as it exists today; all options below are designed to be visually distinct from these.

WIND — current live on site today

Reuses FUN-green / SOLID-gold / WEAK-blue / FLAT-grey from the swell palette. Swell ≡ wind at a glance — that's the confusability problem.

Option A · Cool hue-shift (teal / burnt-orange / slate / warm grey)

Wind sits in different hues from swell entirely. Green-for-good becomes teal, gold becomes burnt orange, blue becomes slate-indigo, grey stays grey but warmer. Same visual weight as swell cells, clearly a different color register.

Option B · Low saturation (same hues, quieter)

Same hue families as swell but desaturated ~40 %. Swell reads as the primary signal, wind recedes to supporting context.

Option C · Hue shift + lighter fills (airy)

Combines A's hue shift with softer background values. Wind cells feel almost like a label strip rather than a data block — good if you want wind framing to feel "meta" vs. swell "data."

Option D · Outlined chip (near-black fill + bright text + thin border)

Wind cells get a common dark background with a vivid colored outline + text — like a tag chip. Visually unambiguous: a filled cell is swell, a chip is wind. Highest-contrast differentiation of the five options.

Option E · Earth tones (sage / ochre / dusty blue / stone)

Wind uses natural muted earth shades throughout. Reads as a warmer, more organic register distinct from swell's digital-bright palette. Softer visual noise on a scroll-heavy page.
