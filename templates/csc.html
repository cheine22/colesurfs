head-to-head · condensed

Core verification columns. Cells are tinted by column — green = best in column, red = worst. Hover a column header for a one-line explanation (full definitions in the glossary).
how to interpret the condensed columns
Hs bias (ft)
Signed mean error of significant wave height. Closer to 0 is better. Positive = model over-predicts the average wave-height of the training window.
Hs MAE (ft)
Average absolute error in feet. Lower is better. Direct, unit-matched quality score — for surfers, each 0.1 ft off the forecast is roughly one category-step risk on a borderline day.
Hs SI
Scatter Index = RMSE ÷ mean observed. Lower is better. Dimensionless; lets you compare a calm-water buoy like Santa Monica Basin against an open-ocean one like Block Island on equal footing. ECMWF's headline wave metric; <0.20 is considered good for Hs.
Skill vs GFS
1 − MSE(model) / MSE(GFS). Higher is better. 0 = ties raw GFS. +0.10 = 10% lower squared error than GFS. Negative = worse than just using raw GFS. The CSC's reason-to-exist check — it has to clear 0 to have earned its keep.
Tp MAE (s)
Mean absolute error of peak period, in seconds. Lower is better. Period bias compounds — a 2 s error in Tp on a long-travel swell shifts expected arrival time by hours.
Tp bias (s)
Signed mean period error. Closer to 0 is better. GFS historically runs slightly long on Tp; the CSC is supposed to pull this back towards zero.
Dp circ MAE (°)
Shortest-arc mean absolute angular error of peak direction. Lower is better. Uses the true compass metric, so 350° vs 10° is an error of 20°, not 340°.
POD >4ft
Probability of detection at Hs > 4 ft. Higher is better (closer to 1). Of all observed hours where seas were over 4 ft, what fraction did the model call correctly?

taylor diagram · Hs (held-out test)

How to read it. Each model is one dot. The OBS reference sits at r=1, normalised σ=1. A model's position packs three numbers into one point:

  • Angle from the horizontal = correlation r with observations. A dot straight along the x-axis → r=1 (perfect tracking). A dot swung up toward the vertical → weaker correlation.
  • Distance from origin = normalised standard deviation. σ=1 means the model's variability matches the observations; <1 = too flat; >1 = too spiky.
  • Distance from the OBS reference point = centered RMSE (dashed arcs). The closer to OBS, the better.

Best possible outcome: a dot right on top of OBS. Watch the CSC winner — it should sit tighter in than raw GFS or raw EURO.

pod / far curves · Hs thresholds

Each line traces one model across Hs thresholds of 2 / 3 / 4 / 6 / 8 / 10 ft. Up-and-left is better: high POD (real events detected), low FAR (few false alarms).
v1 is analysis-level correction only. It predicts the corrected current value at each hour from the two models' analyses at that hour, then applies that same correction at every forecast lead. The forward logger is now running and accumulating real lead-time data (at 24–168 h resolution, one sample per day per lead) for BOTH models. A lead-time-aware CSC v2 will train automatically on that growing archive. Training window starts 2024-03-01 (EURO) / 2024-07-01 (GFS) and extends through today.
