Model definitions

CSC2+baseline — deliberately simple, non-ML correction. Per-[buoy × lead-hour × variable] additive bias on top of the EURO/GFS arithmetic mean (circular mean for direction). No learned interactions, no time-of-year, no EURO–GFS delta. This is the floor the ML model must beat.

CSC2+ML — gradient-boosted trees (LightGBM, one model per output). Inputs per training sample: raw EURO and GFS sw1/sw2 partitions (height, period, sin/cos direction), EURO–GFS delta features per lead (height, period, circular-direction difference), sin/cos day-of-year, buoy identity (one-hot), and lead hour — treated as a first-class feature since every cycle in the archive preserves the full 0..+240 h lead structure. Eight boosters total (sw1/sw2 × {height, period, dp_sin, dp_cos}); direction is recombined via atan2 at inference. GFS rows where partitions are absent reconstitute sw1 from combined-sea fields per the dashboard's v1.7.1 fallback (csc2.train._apply_dashboard_fallback_gfs).

Archive accumulation

Ongoing collection
CMEMS (EURO) forecast cycles are not retained by the provider — every new cycle overwrites the previous one. CSC2 maintains its own forecast archive by logging every 00Z and 12Z cycle across all eight buoys from both EURO (CMEMS) and GFS (Open-Meteo) with the full +0…+240 h lead structure, alongside NDBC buoy observations. The table below stays live even after training so you can see how much the corpus has grown since the model was last trained — retrains happen when the paired-cycle count crosses meaningful thresholds.
A model run is one model initialization tagged by its run_time (e.g. a CMEMS EURO 00Z run on 2025-06-15). Paired model + buoy data counts init times where EURO has a run, GFS has a run, and the buoy has observations at at least 95% of the shared EURO∩GFS forecast valid_utcs. Paired-sample count reported in the sub-label is the actually-matched buoy-hour count, not the whole forecast window. The 95% threshold tolerates routine single-hour NDBC dropouts without letting cycles with large buoy outages slip into training.
