CSC2 · forecast-correction model (data collection in progress)
Corrects EURO (CMEMS) and GFS (Open-Meteo) swell-partition forecasts against NDBC buoy observations. Model training begins once we have accumulated enough paired forecast + observation history.

Archive accumulation

Ongoing collection
CMEMS (EURO) forecast cycles are not retained by the provider — every new cycle overwrites the previous one. CSC2 maintains its own forecast archive by logging every 00Z and 12Z cycle across all eight buoys from both EURO (CMEMS) and GFS (Open-Meteo) with the full +0…+240 h lead structure, alongside NDBC buoy observations. The table below stays live even after training so you can see how much the corpus has grown since the model was last trained — retrains happen when the paired-cycle count crosses meaningful thresholds.
A model run is one model initialization tagged by its run_time (e.g. a CMEMS EURO 00Z run on 2025-06-15). Paired model + buoy data counts init times where EURO has a run, GFS has a run, and the buoy has observations at at least 95% of the shared EURO∩GFS forecast valid_utcs. Paired-sample count reported in the sub-label is the actually-matched buoy-hour count, not the whole forecast window. The 95% threshold tolerates routine single-hour NDBC dropouts without letting cycles with large buoy outages slip into training.

Model definitions

CSC2 (baseline) — a deliberately simple, non-ML correction. Per-[buoy × lead-hour × variable] linear bias correction: subtract the mean training-set error from each raw upstream value. No learned interactions, no time-of-year, no EURO–GFS delta. This is the floor the ML model must beat.

CSC2 (model) — the production gradient-boosted model. Inputs per training sample: raw EURO primary+secondary partitions (Hs/Tp/Dir), raw GFS primary+secondary partitions, EURO–GFS delta features per lead (height, period, circular-direction difference), sin/cos day-of-year, buoy identity (categorical), and lead hour — treated as a first-class feature since every cycle in the archive preserves the full 0..+240 h lead structure. Training loss is computed per (buoy × lead-hour × output), so the model naturally learns how the optimal correction varies as skill degrades with lead time. One model per output variable (6 total), or a joint multi-output head. This is what the dashboard button will serve once trained.
